Frequently Asked Questions

How does massage therapy help me move better and with less pain?

Massage therapy helps you move better by increasing circulation, decreasing muscle tension and reducing pain. When circulation is increased to the tissues it brings in more oxygen and nutrients and helps remove waste products such as lactic acid.

Do I need an appointment, or do you accept walk-ins?
Yes, you will need to book an appointment. All our practitioners are in high demand and you can anticipate booking an appointment approximately 1-2 weeks from now.

You can book your appointment by filling in the short quiz located on this page to be matched with the therapist that’s right for you.
Will I receive treatment on the first day?
Yes, you will get treatment on the first day.

You will fill out an intake form ahead of time that helps give us a headstart on what is going on.

10-20 minutes of verbal and possibly physical assessment to help us with our treatment plan and to help us monitor progress over time.

The remainder of the time will be a hands on treatment.
How long do I have to keep coming in?

Everyone is different, we’ll know better once we can assess you in person.

Are your therapists Certified Registered Massage Therapists (RMTs)?

Yes, all of our therapists are fully trained Registered Massage Therapists (RMTs) that have completed the minimum 2,200 hours of education to receive their certification. They are all recognized by major insurance companies across Alberta.

How do you work with the nervous system?
If someone doesn’t feel safe, calm, or understood, their body cannot relax enough to heal.

We help with:
Calming the system
Reducing emotional overwhelm
Creating an environment where your body feels safe to release tension
Should a massage hurt?

No, a massage should not hurt. For the most part it should feel good, but there are times when we are really trying to make changes in the tissue and it may be a bit uncomfortable. Communication is crucial at these times. Always tell a therapist if it is more pressure than you are comfortable with.

Are athletes the only ones that can benefit from our approach?

No, in fact… most of the people we see are average people that just want to move better and feel better, whether that be getting through work easier or being able to pick their kids up off the floor.

Do I have to talk in a session?
There is always an initial period of understanding, listening with intent on what you’ve experienced so as to understand and tailor your plan.

After that light communication about what you are feeling throughout the session will occur, however, we adapt to whatever level of communication people prefer — silence or conversation are equally welcome.

What is your cancellation policy?

We have a 24 hour cancellation policy to give our therapist a chance to fill that spot. More time is always appreciated but we understand it is not always possible.

Can I claim my massage on my health insurance?

Yes! As long as your therapist is registered (which all ours are), and you have coverage, you can claim your massage for reimbursement through your extended health benefits.

Do you offer direct billing to insurance?

No, however you can claim your massage for reimbursement through your extended health benefits. We send you a digital copy of your receipt that you can use to be reimbursed very quickly.
